Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Unix timestamp? +
A Unix timestamp is the number of seconds (or milliseconds) that have elapsed since January 1, 1970 00:00:00 UTC (the Unix epoch). It's used universally in programming and databases.
How do I know if a timestamp is in seconds or milliseconds? +
Timestamps in seconds are typically 10 digits (e.g. 1716278400). Millisecond timestamps are 13 digits (e.g. 1716278400000). This tool auto-detects which format you're using.
What is ISO 8601? +
ISO 8601 is an international standard for representing dates and times. Example: 2024-05-21T12:00:00.000Z. The Z at the end indicates UTC timezone.
What is the maximum Unix timestamp? +
The maximum 32-bit Unix timestamp is 2,147,483,647 (January 19, 2038). This is the Year 2038 problem. Modern 64-bit systems can represent much larger timestamps.
How do I get the current Unix timestamp? +
Click the Now button to instantly fill in the current Unix timestamp. In code: Date.now() gives milliseconds, Math.floor(Date.now()/1000) gives seconds.
