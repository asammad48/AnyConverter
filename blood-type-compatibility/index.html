Frequently Asked Questions

Why is O− the universal donor? +
O− red blood cells lack A antigens, B antigens, and the Rh factor. This means the recipient's immune system won't recognise them as foreign, making O− safe to transfuse to any blood type in emergencies.
What is the Rh factor? +
The Rh factor is a protein on the surface of red blood cells. If you have it, you are Rh-positive (+). If not, you are Rh-negative (−). Rh-negative people can only receive Rh-negative blood, while Rh-positive people can receive both.
Does blood type compatibility apply to organ transplants? +
This tool specifically covers red blood cell transfusion compatibility. Organ transplant compatibility is more complex and involves additional tissue typing (HLA matching). Always consult medical professionals for transplant decisions.
