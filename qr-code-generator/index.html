Frequently Asked Questions

What can I encode in a QR code? +
You can encode any text string including website URLs, email addresses, phone numbers, plain text messages, Wi-Fi credentials, and more. QR codes support up to several hundred characters depending on the error correction level.
What does the error correction level mean? +
Error correction allows a QR code to be read even when it is partially damaged or obscured. Low (L) corrects up to 7% damage, Medium (M) up to 15%, and High (H) up to 30%. Higher correction produces a denser, more complex code.
Is my data sent to a server? +
No. All QR code generation happens entirely in your browser using JavaScript. Your text or URL is never transmitted to any external server, so your data stays completely private.
What size QR code should I use? +
For web use or sharing online, 256×256 is a good default. For printing on flyers, banners, or business cards, use 512×512 to ensure the code remains scannable even when scaled up or down.
Can I use the generated QR code commercially? +
Yes. QR codes generated with this tool are free for both personal and commercial use. There are no watermarks or restrictions on how you use the downloaded PNG images.
