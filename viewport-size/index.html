Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between viewport and screen resolution? +
Screen resolution is the physical number of pixels your entire monitor has (e.g. 1920×1080). Viewport is the visible area inside your browser window — it changes as you resize the window or scroll bars appear. Viewport is always smaller than or equal to screen resolution.
What is Device Pixel Ratio (DPR)? +
DPR is how many physical pixels make up one CSS pixel. Retina/HiDPI displays have DPR of 2 or higher, meaning 4× more physical pixels per CSS pixel. This is why high-DPI images look sharper on Retina displays.
