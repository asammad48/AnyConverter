How to Use the Percentage Calculator

  1. Step 1 — Choose the calculation mode that matches your question: "X% of Y", "X is what % of Y", "% Change", or "% Increase/Decrease".
  2. Step 2 — Enter the required values in the input fields that appear for your selected mode.
  3. Step 3 — Click "Calculate" to see your result instantly. Click "Reset" to clear the fields and start a new calculation.

Features

  • Four calculation modes covering the most common percentage problems
  • X% of Y: find a percentage value (e.g., 25% of 200 = 50)
  • What percent: determine how one number relates to another as a percentage
  • Percentage change: see the exact increase or decrease between two values
  • Increase/Decrease: apply a percentage to a base value to get the new amount
  • Results displayed with clear labels explaining what was calculated

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate what percentage one number is of another? +
Select the "X is what % of Y" mode, enter your value (X) and the total (Y), then click Calculate. The formula used is (X ÷ Y) × 100. For example, 50 is 25% of 200.
How is percentage change calculated? +
Percentage change = ((New Value − Original Value) ÷ |Original Value|) × 100. A positive result means an increase; a negative result means a decrease. For example, going from 100 to 150 is a +50% change.
What is the difference between percentage increase and percentage change? +
Percentage change compares two given values to find how much one changed relative to the other. Percentage increase/decrease takes a single value and applies a percentage to it to calculate the new resulting value after the increase or decrease.
Can I calculate a discount using this tool? +
Yes. Use the "% Increase/Decrease" mode, enter the original price as the Value, enter the discount percentage, click "Decrease by %", then Calculate. The result shows the final price after the discount has been applied.
Does this calculator handle decimal percentages? +
Yes. You can enter decimal values in any field, such as 7.5% or 12.25. The calculator handles all decimal inputs and displays results with up to four decimal places, trimming trailing zeros for readability.
