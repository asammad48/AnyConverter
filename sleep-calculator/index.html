How to Use the Sleep Calculator

  1. To find wake-up times: Enter your bedtime and click "Calculate Wake-Up". You'll see 4 options (3–6 sleep cycles) with the optimal ones highlighted.
  2. To find bedtimes: Enter your required wake-up time and click "Calculate Bedtime". Choose a bedtime that gives you 5 or 6 full cycles.
  3. Tip: The calculator adds 14 minutes for the average time it takes to fall asleep.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why are sleep cycles 90 minutes? +
Research shows that a typical human sleep cycle (light sleep → deep sleep → REM sleep) takes about 90 minutes to complete. Waking up at the end of a cycle, rather than in the middle, leaves you feeling more alert and refreshed.
How many cycles should I aim for? +
Most adults need 5–6 complete sleep cycles per night, which equals 7.5–9 hours of sleep. The calculator highlights these as "Recommended" options.
Why is 14 minutes added? +
Studies show it takes the average person about 14 minutes to fall asleep after getting into bed. The calculator adds this buffer so the cycle count starts from when you actually fall asleep, not when you lie down.
