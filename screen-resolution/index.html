Frequently Asked Questions

What is Device Pixel Ratio (DPR)? +
DPR is the ratio of physical pixels to logical (CSS) pixels. Retina and HiDPI displays have a DPR of 2 or higher, meaning a 1280×800 logical viewport actually uses 2560×1600 physical pixels.
Why is viewport different from screen resolution? +
Screen resolution is the total physical pixels of your monitor. Viewport is the visible area inside the browser window, which is smaller because it excludes toolbars, scrollbars, and OS chrome.
Is my screen data sent anywhere? +
No. All screen data is read directly from your browser's JavaScript APIs (screen, window, navigator) and displayed locally. Nothing is sent to any server.
