Frequently Asked Questions

What is BMI and how is it calculated? +
BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure of body fat based on your weight and height. The formula is: BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²). For imperial units, the tool converts your values to metric before calculating.
What are the BMI categories? +
The standard BMI categories are: Underweight (below 18.5), Normal weight (18.5–24.9), Overweight (25–29.9), and Obese (30 and above). These ranges are defined by the World Health Organization.
Is BMI an accurate measure of health? +
BMI is a useful screening tool but does not directly measure body fat. Athletes may have a high BMI due to muscle mass, while older adults may have a normal BMI with a high body fat percentage. Always consult a healthcare professional for a comprehensive health assessment.
How does the healthy weight range work? +
The healthy weight range shown is the weight range that corresponds to a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 for your specific height. It is displayed in the same units you selected (kg or lb).
Can children use this BMI calculator? +
This calculator uses standard adult BMI thresholds. BMI for children and teens is interpreted differently using age- and sex-specific percentiles. For anyone under 18, please use a pediatric BMI calculator or consult a doctor.
