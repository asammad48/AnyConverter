How to Use the Typing Speed Test

  1. Step 1 — Select your preferred difficulty level: Easy, Medium, or Hard.
  2. Step 2 — Click "Start Test" and begin typing the displayed text in the input box. The timer starts automatically when you type your first character.
  3. Step 3 — Type as accurately and quickly as you can. When the 60-second timer ends, your WPM, accuracy, correct words, and errors are displayed.

Features

  • Three difficulty levels: Easy (common words), Medium (programming), and Hard (technical vocabulary)
  • Real-time WPM counter updates as you type
  • Color-coded text highlights correct characters in green and errors in red
  • 60-second timed test with live countdown
  • Detailed results: WPM, accuracy percentage, correct words, and total errors
  • No signup required — works entirely in your browser

Frequently Asked Questions

What is WPM and how is it calculated? +
WPM stands for Words Per Minute. In this test, each correctly typed word completed within the 60-second window counts as one WPM. This is the standard "gross WPM" measurement used by most typing tests.
What is a good typing speed? +
The average typist achieves 40–50 WPM. A speed of 60–80 WPM is considered above average and suitable for most office jobs. Professional typists often exceed 80–100 WPM, while competitive typists can reach 120+ WPM.
How does the accuracy percentage work? +
Accuracy is calculated based on the ratio of correctly typed characters to total characters typed. Each mistyped character reduces your accuracy score. Aim for at least 95% accuracy for professional typing standards.
Can I retake the test with a different text? +
Yes. After your results appear, click "Try Again" to return to the setup screen. Each time you start a new test, a random passage is selected from the available texts for your chosen difficulty level.
Does the test work on mobile devices? +
Yes, the typing speed test works on mobile devices and tablets with a hardware keyboard. However, for the most accurate WPM measurement, a physical keyboard is recommended, as on-screen keyboards typically produce slower speeds due to their nature.
