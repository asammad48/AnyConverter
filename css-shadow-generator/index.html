Frequently Asked Questions

What is the "Spread" value in box-shadow? +
Spread expands or contracts the shadow beyond the blur radius. A positive spread makes the shadow larger than the element; a negative spread makes it smaller and tighter, creating a more realistic depth effect.
What does "Inset" do? +
When checked, the box-shadow is rendered inside the element rather than outside. This creates a pressed-in or embossed look, commonly used for sunken input fields or pressed buttons.
Can text-shadow use spread or inset? +
No. The CSS text-shadow property only accepts horizontal offset, vertical offset, and blur radius. Spread and inset are only valid for box-shadow.
