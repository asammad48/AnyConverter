Frequently Asked Questions

How does vinculum notation work for numbers above 3,999? +
Vinculum notation places a bar (overline) over a Roman numeral to multiply its value by 1,000. So V̄ = 5,000, X̄ = 10,000, L̄ = 50,000, C̄ = 100,000, D̄ = 500,000, M̄ = 1,000,000. For example, 4,000 is written MV̄ (5,000 − 1,000) and 2,500,000 is written M̄M̄D̄.
What is subtractive notation? +
Subtractive notation means a smaller numeral placed before a larger one is subtracted. For example, IV = 5−1 = 4, and CM = 1000−100 = 900. This avoids IIII for 4 or VIIII for 9.
Can I convert the current year? +
Yes. Enter the current year (e.g. 2026) and it will convert to MMXXVI. Any year between 1 and 3,999,999 is supported — including historical dates and future projections.
