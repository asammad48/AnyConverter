Frequently Asked Questions

What is the standard tip percentage? +
In the United States, the standard tip percentage is typically 15–20% for restaurant service. 15% is considered adequate, 18% is good, and 20% or more is excellent. For exceptional service, many people tip 25% or more.
How do I calculate a tip without a calculator? +
To calculate a 20% tip mentally, move the decimal point one place left (10%), then double it. For a $45 bill: 10% = $4.50, doubled = $9.00 tip. For 15%, calculate 10% and add half of that amount.
Should I tip on the pre-tax or post-tax total? +
Most etiquette experts suggest tipping on the pre-tax subtotal, as taxes are government-mandated and not related to the service. However, tipping on the full post-tax amount is also widely accepted and appreciated by service staff.
How does the bill splitting feature work? +
Enter the number of people sharing the bill in the "Number of People" field. The calculator automatically divides the total bill (including tip) equally among all people and displays the per-person amount in the "Per Person" card.
Can I tip a custom percentage? +
Yes. Use the slider below the preset buttons to set any tip percentage from 0% to 50%. The percentage display updates in real time as you drag, and all three result cards update instantly to reflect your chosen percentage.
