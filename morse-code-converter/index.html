How to Use Morse Code Converter

  1. Step 1 — Choose "Text → Morse" to encode or "Morse → Text" to decode.
  2. Step 2 — Type or paste your input in the left panel. Conversion happens as you type.
  3. Step 3 — Click "Copy Output" to copy the Morse code or decoded text.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I decode Morse code? +
Click "Morse → Text", then enter dots (.) and dashes (-) separated by spaces between letters. Use a forward slash ( / ) to separate words. For example: ".... . .-.. .-.. --- / .-- --- .-. .-.. -.." decodes to "HELLO WORLD".
What characters are supported? +
All 26 English letters (A-Z), digits 0-9, and common punctuation: period, comma, question mark, exclamation, slash, parentheses, ampersand, colon, semicolon, equals, plus, underscore, quotes, dollar, and at symbol.
Why is the output uppercase? +
Morse code itself has no concept of case — there are no separate Morse symbols for uppercase and lowercase letters. Standard convention is to decode Morse to uppercase, though you can change the case afterward if needed.
What does the "/" mean in Morse? +
In this tool, a forward slash surrounded by spaces ( / ) represents the word separator. When encoding, spaces between words become " / ". When decoding, use " / " to separate words in your Morse input.
